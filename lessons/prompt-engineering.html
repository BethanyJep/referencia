📖 Introduction: What is Prompt Engineering?

Imagine a cat 🐱 has found a genie 🧞 who grants wishes. The cat says "I want milk!" — and a milk truck crashes through the wall! 🚛💥 The cat should have said: "I want a small bowl of warm milk, served in my favorite dish, please."

Prompt Engineering is the art of communicating with AI to get exactly what you want. Like wishing carefully with a genie, how you phrase your request dramatically affects the output!

💡 Key Insight

Prompt Engineering is crafting inputs to AI models that elicit the best possible outputs. It's about being specific, providing context, and guiding the AI toward your desired result!

"Prompting: Ask the genie carefully, or get a milk truck!"

🎯 What You'll Learn

  • Why prompts matter so much for LLM outputs
  • Core techniques: specificity, examples, personas, chain-of-thought
  • Prompt patterns that work across different tasks
  • Common mistakes and how to avoid them
  • Advanced techniques: few-shot, RAG, prompt chaining

🐱 The Genie Wish Story

Moral: LLMs are like literal genies—they do exactly what you ask, not what you meant!

Vague wish: "Make me rich" → You're now named Rich 🤦

Specific wish: "Transfer $1 million to my bank account at [Bank], account #[X], from a legal source, without anyone being harmed" → Better! (though still ethically questionable 😅)

Lesson: Be specific, provide context, and anticipate edge cases!

📚 Core Content

🎯 Be Specific

Bad: "Write about cats"

Good: "Write a 200-word blog post about why cats make great pets for apartment living"

📋 Provide Context

Bad: "Fix this code"

Good: "This Python function should sort a list, but it's returning None. Here's the code: [code]"

📐 Specify Format

Bad: "List some ideas"

Good: "Give me 5 ideas as a numbered list, each with a one-sentence explanation"

🎭 Set the Tone

Bad: "Explain quantum physics"

Good: "Explain quantum physics in a fun, casual way like you're talking to a curious 10-year-old"

🎭 Role/Persona Prompting

Tell the AI who to "be":

"You are an experienced Python developer who writes clean, well-documented code. 
Review this code and suggest improvements..."

🧠 Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Ask the AI to show its reasoning:

"Solve this step by step:
A store has 50 apples. They sell 23 and receive a shipment of 15. How many do they have?

Think through each step before giving the final answer."

This dramatically improves accuracy on complex reasoning tasks!

📚 Few-Shot Learning

Show examples of what you want:

"Classify the sentiment as positive, negative, or neutral.

Example 1: "I love this product!" → Positive
Example 2: "Terrible experience, never again" → Negative  
Example 3: "It arrived on Tuesday" → Neutral

Now classify: "This exceeded my expectations!"

📋 Output Templates

Give a structure to fill:

"Analyze this product and respond in this format:

PROS:
- [list 3 pros]

CONS:
- [list 3 cons]

VERDICT: [one sentence summary]"

"Act as..." Pattern

Act as a senior software engineer...
Act as a helpful tutor who...
Act as a creative writing coach...

"Let's think step by step"

[Complex problem]

Let's think through this step by step.

Critique & Improve

Here's my [work]. First, identify 3 weaknesses.
Then provide specific improvements for each.

Pros/Cons Analysis

Analyze [X] by listing:
- 5 advantages
- 5 disadvantages
- Overall recommendation

❌ Being Too Vague

"Write something about AI"

→ Gets generic, unfocused output

❌ Missing Context

"Is this a good approach?"

→ AI doesn't know your goals/constraints

❌ One Giant Prompt

Asking for 10 things at once

→ Quality drops, things get missed

❌ No Examples

Expecting specific format without showing it

→ Output doesn't match expectations

✅ The Fix: Iterative Refinement

Don't expect perfection on the first try! Prompting is iterative:

  1. Try a prompt
  2. Evaluate the output
  3. Identify what's missing or wrong
  4. Refine the prompt
  5. Repeat until satisfied

🔗 Prompt Chaining

Break complex tasks into multiple prompts:

  1. Prompt 1: "Brainstorm 10 blog post ideas about AI"
  2. Prompt 2: "Pick the best idea and create an outline"
  3. Prompt 3: "Write the introduction based on this outline"
  4. ...

Each step focuses on one thing → better quality!

📚 RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

Inject relevant documents into the prompt:

Based on the following documentation:
[Retrieved relevant docs]

Answer this question: [User question]

Only use information from the provided documentation.

Reduces hallucination by grounding answers in real data!

🎯 System Prompts

Set persistent instructions for the entire conversation:

System: You are a helpful coding assistant. Always:
- Write clean, documented code
- Explain your reasoning
- Suggest best practices
- Ask clarifying questions when needed
